\section{Question}
In the figure given below O is the centre of the circle. If QR = OP  and  $\angle$ ORP = 20. Find the value of x giving reasons
\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[]{assignment 1 figure.jpg}
\end{center}
\section{solution}
Let the radius of the circle be r.

given OP = OR,

OP = OQ = QR = r,

IN $\Delta$ OQR, OQ = QR
$\implies$
$\angle$ QOR = $\angle$ ORP = 20
\\Consider $\Delta$ OQR , An exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the two opposite interior angles//
And $\angle$ OQP = $\angle$ QOR = $\angle$ ORQ
$\angle$ OQP = 20 + 20
    $\implies$
$\angle$ OQP = 40
\hline
In , $\Delta$ OPQ 
\\Sum of all angles in a triangle is equal to 180//
   $\implies$
$\angle$ POQ = 180 - ($\angle$ OPQ + $\angle$ OQP)
$\implies$
$\angle$ POQ = 180 - 40 - 40
    $\implies$
$\angle$ POQ = 100
\hline
\\Sum of angle of striaght line is 180//
 $implies$
$\angle$ x + $\angle$ POQ + $\angle$ QOR = 180
  $\implies$
$\angle$ x + 100 + 20 = 180$.
  $\implies$
\angle$ x = 60
