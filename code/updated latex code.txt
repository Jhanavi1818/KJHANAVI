\section{Question}
In the figure given below O is the centre of the circle. If QR = OP  and  $\angle$ ORP = 20. Find the value of x giving reasons
\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[]{assignment 1 figure.jpg}
\end{center}
\section{solution}
\\Let the radius of the circle be r.//

given OP = OR,

OP = OQ = QR = r,

IN $\Delta$ OQR, OQ = QR

$\angle$ QOR = $\angle$ ORP = 20
\\Consider $\Delta$ OQR , An exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the two opposite interior angles//

\begin{center}
    $\angle$ OQP = $\angle$ QOR + $\angle$ ORQ
\end{center}
\begin{center}
    $ \rightarrow \angle OQP = 20+20$
\end{center}
   \begin{equation}
       \rightarrow \angle OQP = 40
   \end{equation}
 \\consider $\Delta$ OPQ 
\\Sum of all angles in a triangle is equal to 180//
\begin{center}
    $ \rightarrow \angle POQ = 180 - (\angle OPQ + \angle OQP)$
    \end{center}
\begin{center}
   $ \rightarrow \angle POQ = 180-40-40$
\end{center}
\begin{equation}
    \rightarrow \angle POQ = 100
\end{equation}
\\Sum of angle of straight line is 180//
 \begin{center}
     $\rightarrow \angle x + \angle POQ + \angle QOR =  180$
 \end{center}
 \begin{center}
     $\rightarrow \angle x +100+20 = 180$
 \end{center}
 \begin{equation}
     \rightarrow \angle x =60
 \end{equation}
therefore $\angle$ x is 60 when OQ = QR
and hence x = 60
