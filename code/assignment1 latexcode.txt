\textbf {Question 10b:}
 
    \textbf{Question:}
In the figure given below O is the centre of the circle. If QR = OP  and  $\angle$ ORP = 20. Find the value of x giving reasons
\textbf{solution}
\\Let the radius of the circle be r.//

given OP = OR,

OP = OQ = QR = r,

IN $\Delta$ OQR, OQ = QR

$\angle$ QOR = $\angle$ ORP = 20
\\Consider $\Delta$ OQR , An exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the two opposite interior angles//
\begin{align}
    \begin{center}
    $\angle$ OQP = $\angle$ QOR + $\angle$ ORQ
\end{center}
   \begin{equation}
       \rightarrow \angle OQP = 40
   \end{equation}
    \end{align}
    \\consider $\Delta$ OPQ 
    \begin{center}
    $\angle$ POQ = 180 - $\angle$OPQ -$\angle$OQP
    $\implies$ 180 - 40 - 40
        \end{center}
     \begin{equation}
    \rightarrow \angle POQ = 100
\end{equation}
now, $\angle$x +$\angle$POQ +$\angle$QOR = 180
\\A straight line//
$\begin{center}
    $\angle$ x +100+20 = 180
\end{center}$
$\angle$x = 180-120 = 60

hence, the value of x is 60
